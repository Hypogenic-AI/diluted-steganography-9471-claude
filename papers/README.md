# Downloaded Papers

## Core Papers (Directly Related to Hypothesis)

1. **NEST: Nascent Encoded Steganographic Thoughts** (2602.14095_nest_stego_thoughts.pdf)
   - Authors: Artem Karpov
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2602.14095
   - Why relevant: Most directly relevant - systematically evaluates acrostic steganography across 28 models, showing performance degradation with sequence length (a form of signal dilution)

2. **Early Signs of Steganographic Capabilities in Frontier LLMs** (2507.02737_early_signs_stego_capabilities.pdf)
   - Authors: Zolkowski, Nishimura-Gasparian, McCarthy, Zimmermann, Lindner
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2507.02737
   - Why relevant: Tests multiple encoding schemes including `sentence_letter` (concentrated) vs `third_word_letter` (diluted), providing direct evidence for dilution effects. Open-source evaluation suite.

3. **The Steganographic Potentials of Language Models** (2505.03439_steganographic_potentials_lm.pdf)
   - Authors: Karpov, Adeleke, Cho, Perez-Campanero
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2505.03439
   - Why relevant: RL fine-tuning for steganography, demonstrates performance degradation with payload length increase, categorizes steganographic capabilities
